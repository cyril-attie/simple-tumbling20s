The Banking Hack Manual

Origininally, when money products (mainly gold and silver) was deposited into the bank an equivalent amount of deposit bills was issued to the depositor. But afterwards, through credit, additional bills were poured into circulation without gold and silver being deposited at the bank in counterpart. When granting credit, rather than being backed by gold bills are backed by a debt contract. A debt contract is a promise to pay back the borrowed amount plus accumulated interests over time. The banking credit makes bill-denominated debt unpayable and bills quantity ever increasing making them a very bad way to store wealth, therefore a very bad money product substitute. Banking credit consequences are:

  1. Bank deposit bills are not anymore a money. Because bills are not fully backed by money products they are excluded from the original 'best trading strategy' of trading leftovers for money products as for example gold.
  2. Banking credit makes impossible to pay back the debt. Indeed, the debt contract says every year an interest is to be added on the previous-year debt-balance. In other words the debt-balance multiplies itself every year by (1+i) where i is the interest rate. But the number of bank bills poured into circulation at the time of the credit stays the same, it does not increase or multiply. Therefore after some years the total debt balances of outstanding credits overcome the total existing bills making it forever unfeasible to payback the credit debt owed to the banks because there is not enough circulating bills to pay that amount. Clever trick, I call it the banking hack.
  3. Bankers, and their network, hide the hack through what I call The Banking Credit Cycle, which is alternating periods of inflation, Crisis, deflation and reflation:
    1. Inflation: Monetary inflation refers to increasing (or inflating) quantity of circulating bills because of credit granting. Monetary inflation spurs product price inflation and speculation. When product price inflation becomes too noticeable banks rise interest rates to earn more revenue and to discourage businessmen and speculators to take new credits.
    2. Crisis: At a higher interest rate speculators withdraw from markets and prices that were getting inflated pop. In other words, interest rate rise kicks off a Crisis, that is when over-inflated prices fall more than 50% in less than 3 years. During Crisis almost every smart debtor is looking to redeem its debt. And debt redemption equates to monetary deflation.
    3. Deflation: monetary deflation refers to diminishing (or deflating) quantity of circulating bills because of credit redemption. Monetary deflation comes along product price deflation and panic. Indeed falling product prices means one will have to sell more products to raise the same amount of debt. Falling product prices means people is encouraged to save money instead of consuming as tomorrow products will be cheaper than today, and that makes it even more difficult to raise enough money to pay off the debt. Product prices deflation is the scariest nightmare of indebted businessmen that collaborate with bankers. That is why the cycle closes by with lowering again interest rates to encourage credit taking in order to reflate the quantity of bills and prices.
    4. Reflation: As long as there is not a corresponding monetary reflation to prevent price deflation the circulating bills drain to the banks allowing them to acquire assets at a deflated (Crisis) price. Monetary reflation is the quanitty of circulating bills re-increasing after having diminished. The re-increase is achieved by lowering interest rates to encourage credt taking. Finally big businessmen, corporations or the government take big credits to reflate the quantity of circulating bills, the prices follow and the economic activity. Coming back to the base point: inflation.

So The Banking Credit Cycle describes how bankers distract people generating sensationalist news through credit-pumped speculative bubbles and subsequent panicking Crises.

Once more, all this explanaition is meant to understand how debt is unpayable and why the quantity of bank bills increases exponentially, and therefore are a bad product money substitute for smart trader strategy. To sum up:

  1. Every credit redemption is greater than every previously taken credit because of accumulated interest over time
  2. Banks are the biggest debtors as they owe the all the deposits to their clients and debtors are really afraid of price deflation
  3. In order to keep product prices steadily increasing every new credit must be greater than or equal to the previous credit redemption that was already greater than the previous credit
  4. The previous point leads to an exponential increase (the next increase is always greater than the previous, see Fig. 1) of the quantity of bills, making the banking bill a very poor money substitute as the bill loses all its value over time (see Fig. 2)

The Last Two Cycles 2000 and 2008

For example, in 2000 and in 2008 we have two perfect Crisis of Banking Credit Cycles. After the 60's agricultural revolution and 70'S inflation came a 20 years period of stability where productivity was steadily increasing thanks to the widespread adoption of productivity and competitiveness techniques.

In 1998, spiking oil prices warned bankers of undergoing price inflation. Bankers raised interest rates to discourage people from speculating and taking credit but the 'dot com' bubble was already under way. Stock market prices (NASDAQ 100 Index) that had doubled in the previous 2 years doubled twice more in the next 2 years: from 562pts in 1996, to 1200pts in 1998 and to 4800pts in 2000. When the crisis started, after 2 years of rising interest rates and monetary deflation, nobody expected stock prices to fall 80%. Rather the opposite, polled people generally answered the stock market would keep climbing to new maxima. The public explained this irrational prejudice because of the new millenia and Internet revolution, but only bankers and their network were aware of monetary deflation and undergoing crisis coming. In the next 3 years from 2000 till 2003 the index fell back under 900pts level. Can you imagine the euphoria while your savings multiply by 8 in 5 years and the subsequent panic when you lose 80% in 2 years? That's how bankers distract the public to their advantage: only bankers know when bills are being drained out of or poured in circulation, but they play with news to trick uninformed players. In 2006, again after 2 years of rising interest rates, house prices topped and started to fall after having more than doubled in 4 years. By buying government debt directly in the market central bankers changed the rules of the game, they bypassed the need of a credit taker to reflate the economy. Usually a big institution like corporations or the government are the credit takers profiting from deflated crisis prices, but this time it was not enough to compensate for credit redemption.
